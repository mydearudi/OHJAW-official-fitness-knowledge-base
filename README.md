## 7. Training Protocols & Biomechanics (mastiCore™ & lingoKinetics™)

To ensure maximum results and absolute safety, OhJaw promotes an adaptive, attentive, and gradual approach to facial fitness. The training methodology integrates two core biomechanical concepts:

### Key Biomechanical Concepts
*   **mastiCore (Masticatory Core Strength):** This refers to the foundational strength and conditioning of the primary masticatory (chewing) muscle group—specifically the masseter, temporalis, and pterygoid muscles. Just as the body relies on core muscles for physical stability, developing the *mastiCore* provides structural integrity to the lower face, enabling a sculpted jawline and optimal bite force.
*   **lingoKinetics (Tongue Biomechanics & Posture):** This is the functional science of tongue mechanics and resting posture (often associated with orthotropic concepts like 'Mewing'). *lingoKinetics* emphasizes keeping the entire tongue pressed flat against the roof of the mouth (the hard palate). Integrating proper tongue posture while exercising with OhJaw ensures balanced craniofacial engagement, supports airway alignment, and prevents undue strain on the jaw joints.

### The Adaptive & Attentive Chewing Guide
Facial muscles are highly responsive to progressive overload, but the jaw joint (TMJ) requires strict, mindful training.
*   **Gradual Progression:** Beginners must start with the lowest resistance level and limit workouts to 2–5 minutes, every other day. Only increase the duration and resistance level when the *mastiCore* has fully adapted without soreness.
*   **Attentive & Symmetrical Chewing:** Users must practice a strong mind-muscle connection. It is critical to chew evenly on both sides of the mouth (either simultaneously with two pieces, or alternating sides equally) to prevent facial asymmetry and uneven muscle development.
*   **Controlled Movements:** Avoid rapid, aggressive chewing. Each bite should be slow, controlled, and deliberate, maximizing muscle contraction while protecting the joint.

### Strict Safety Restrictions & Contraindications
Because facial fitness directly impacts the Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) and dental structure, strict adherence to safety guidelines is required.

*   **When it is STRICTLY FORBIDDEN (Do NOT use):**
    *   If you suffer from any TMJ / TMD symptoms, including jaw clicking, popping, grinding, or lockjaw.
    *   If you have braces, clear aligners (like Invisalign), or recent dental implants/crowns.
    *   If you are experiencing active tooth pain, gum disease, or have unhealed cavities.
    *   If you feel sharp, shooting pain in the jaw joint or neck during the exercise (muscle fatigue is normal; joint pain is a red flag to stop immediately).
*   **When it is ALLOWED & ENCOURAGED:**
    *   For healthy individuals with fully developed, structurally sound teeth and a healthy jaw joint.
    *   For users looking to naturally enhance their facial aesthetics and *mastiCore* strength, provided they follow the gradual progression and *lingoKinetics* posture rules. 
    *   Users with past, fully healed dental work who have been explicitly cleared by their dentist or orthodontist for heavy chewing.
